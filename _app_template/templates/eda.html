Feature dictionary

The tabular tier is the standard GTZAN 58-feature vector: length + 28 audio descriptors, each summarised by its mean and variance across the clip. The full per-feature distributions, stats, and per-genre splits live in the Distributions tab — this dictionary defines what each one measures.

featurewhat it measurescolumns
length Number of audio samples in the clip — a fixed-rate proxy for duration. Constant for clean 30 s files; deviations flag the off-duration clips noted above. 1
chroma_stft ·mean/var Chromagram from the short-time Fourier transform — energy folded into the 12 pitch classes. Captures harmony / key content independent of octave. 2
rms ·mean/var Root-mean-square energy per frame — overall loudness / dynamics. Variance separates steady tracks from ones with big swells and drops. 2
spectral_centroid ·mean/var The spectrum's "center of mass" — perceived brightness. Higher for bright/treble-heavy genres, lower for bass-dominant ones. 2
spectral_bandwidth ·mean/var Spread of energy around the centroid — how wide vs. focused the spectrum is at each frame. 2
rolloff ·mean/var The frequency below which a set fraction (~85%) of spectral energy lies — a skew/edge descriptor of the spectral shape. 2
zero_crossing_rate ·mean/var How often the waveform crosses zero — tracks noisiness / percussiveness; high for distorted or unpitched sounds, low for smooth tones. 2
harmony ·mean/var Energy of the harmonic (pitched, tonal) component after harmonic–percussive separation. 2
perceptr ·mean/var "Perceptr" = the percussive component — transient, rhythmic energy, the complement to harmony. 2
tempo Estimated beats per minute from onset/autocorrelation. A single global value per clip. 1
mfcc1 … mfcc20 ·mean/var Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients — a compact description of the spectral envelope (timbre). Low orders track gross shape; higher orders capture fine detail. The dominant block of the vector (40 of 58 columns). 40
All descriptors except length and tempo appear as a mean/variance pair (28 × 2 = 56) → 56 + length + tempo = 58 features. The 3 s tier computes the same 58 over each 3-second segment.
